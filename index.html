I2P - Anonymizing Network Layer

There are a lot of I2P evangelists out there which makes me wonder how good the protocol is. TOR has been around for quite a long time now, and this may just be some level of paranoia talking, but I feel that TOR isn't as good as it used to be.

I have to admit that I'm not greatly interested in the dark web, but I feel that I am often coming across a headline or whitepaper that claims to have found a weakness in TOR, its relays, services, what-have-you, or have found a way to de-anonymize its users.

There are more protocols out there beyond both TOR & I2P and they would have presumably learned from any mistakes garnered by the TOR project. What's more, it seems that many developers are showing much more urgency and forward-thinking when considering the advent of quantum computing. Either way, if privacy and/or anonymity is the goal, its usually best to be changing ones habits -- Translation: If you've been using TOR and your favorite cryptocurrency for awhile, it's probably time to expand your portfolio.

P.S. I would also like to add that I am mostly disappointed with the dark web community. They are rarely the infosec experts they think they are.

For every DeSnake or ex-phreaker on the dark web, there's a thousand more wanna-be's. Not saying I'm better than anyone or anything, but I know my limits and most of these self-described hackers (ugh, hate that term these days) are totally rote and ineffective.
