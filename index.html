Fun Fact...

Yeah, fun fact, after using various IDEs for years, my workflow/build process is just Sublime for text editing, cli compilers (mostly gcc), build languages like make or cmake, et al.

There's totally legit reasons for not doing it this way and letting a nice IDE like Visual Studio/VS-Code do the heavy-lifting behind the scenes, but I will say that this way definitely helps one's understanding of said processes. The compilation process alone is incredibly complicated; several phases & tools are required to convert high-level source code into executable instructions (Lexical analysis, tokenization, linking, assembly).

If anyone's interested in software compilation specifically, I would easily give my endorsement to the legendary "Compilers: Principles, Techniques, & Tools," co-authored by the equally legendary Al Aho of AT&T Bell Labs fame.

About

Programming is an amazing skill, which helps further my understanding of systems and networking design & principles. On the hardware side, it's always rewarding to be able to help a friend build a PC from scratch to save a few dollars - and in high school, this meant I had another person to play starcraft with online (56kbps!).

Currently a college dropout and mostly auto-educated. Read as many books from No Starch & O'Reilly publications as you can...
