Fun Fact...

Yeah, fun fact, after using various IDEs for years, my workflow/build process is just Sublime for text editing, cli compilers & tools, build languages like make or cmake, et al. If you can't tell, I prefer Unix-like (especially Debian-based Linux distros) platforms for development and would rather use mingw to compile & link the necessary headers for Windows. However, if I have to, I still have a dedicated, Windows 10 desktop with Visual Studio & MSVC, Windows Terminal, Powershell, WSL (Win Subsystem for Linux), et al.

There's totally legit reasons for not doing it this way and letting a nice IDE do the heavy-lifting behind the scenes, but I will say that this way definitely helps one's understanding of said processes.

If anyone's interested in software compilation specifically, I would easily give my endorsement to the legendary "Compilers: Principles, Techniques, & Tools," co-authored by the equally legendary Al Aho of AT&T Bell Labs fame.
