Truthfully, I don't think I really enjoy front-end development,
like designing a static web page or dynamic web application. I really
like InfoSec, although it is a very intimidating, very multi-disciplinary
type of field with a LOT of competition these days; I was actually hoping
that making a website would help me see things from the "other side," I.e.,
I wanted to see if I could make a website and learn some best practices for
creating something on the web, rather than trying to break it.

That's why if you get into the industry of IT or InfoSec, you will probably
end up hearing that developers make good hackers at some point; again, those
who are good at creating the front and back ends of web sites often know the
best course of action for pentesting or other security assessments.

Even so, I was thinking that, since I already have gotten familiar with Markdown
and writing Jupyter notebooks, I could just throw those up on here and
continue adding tutorials, ctf write-ups, etc. As I continue to learn and get better
with web tech, I would like to write easy-to-understand tutorials for beginners as well
as contribute more in-depth discussion towards more advanced topics.

There's lots to do and I hope to share thorough, quality information.
See you soon!!

I love the X Files!

Anybody else out there watching TV over the air? My family has a couple
antennas, so I will watch it from time to time. I think it's amazing how
I still prefer not having control of my entertainment sometimes, rather than
being able to choose when, where, and how you watch or stream anything these
days.

This summer I started watching this channel Comet, which has this very awe-
some, very nostalgic programming consisting of Buffy the Vampire Slayer and
The X Files.

Holy crap, I'm in love with the X Files.

As I child, I actually grew to strongly dislike shows like The X Files,
Star Trek, Nick @ Night, etc. because, they pretty much marked
the end of the TV that I liked, which was of course cartoons.

Haha, but I digress, what an amazing show. Agents Mulder & Scully balance each other
so well -- Mulder, who has a poster in his office that perfectly sums him up, that reads
"I Want to Believe", and Scully, who is as pragmatic as she is intelligent.
Together, they exemplify both the fringe and the level-headed, scientific approaches.

I started streaming the series, starting at season 1. I may create an area on the site
for entertainment-related content. If I do, I'd like to comment more on the show and its
themes, cast, story, et al. Seems to be a lot there as well. There's a couple movies, some
video games, and the show itself is 11 seasons. It also seems to take a turn for a more
comedic tone while also making various statements on political & current events.

Either way, I'm hoping to start to develop the site further; both in terms of code and content.
