Programming Experience

Just really quickly, I'd like to talk a little bit about my programming background.

I have been an avid, strong computer user, basically, since I was born. I fondly remember cutting & pasting HTML code from tutorials and into my free website, hosted by Angelfire (Still around!) when I was in 5th grade. Of course, I was an accomplished PC gamer and built myself and my friends custom gaming PCs. We ordered the parts off of a website I found, called Newegg.com (the one-and-only) and we played games like Starcraft and Counter-Strike. I guess I have to mention, we were among the very first Steam users when we transitioned from CS 1.5 to version 1.6.

I had begun to get serious about computers & programming when I decided to enroll at my local community college as a computer science major (CS121). There, I started learning Cpp and Object-Oriented Programming paradigms, but more importantly, I started learning how a computer "thinks" and solves problems. I continued to write in C++, exclusively, for years. I made many console-based apps and eventually scratched the surface of development libraries like SDL2, SFML, and FLTK for GUI's and games.

Finally, I started branching out into other languages. I realized that, a good programmer isn't limited by any single, high-level programming languages; one should be able to become proficient in absolutely any language after learning the necessary fundamentals. After learning your third language or so, you start to feel this confidence.

My second language, Python, was an excellent choice and I was very much enjoying the immediate feedback given by an interpreted language. It's so quick & easy to just run the interpreter (iPython, because I'm a huge CLI-user) and try something out. You can indulge even the most trivial of curiosities with very little effort.

My current muse is golang; the Go programming language. Firstly, I'm a fan of Ken Thompson, who co-authored the language with two other geniuses. Go is a fantastic programming language. In my opinion, it succeeded, in spades, its project's goal, which was to create a modern, efficient, scalable high-level programming language with its own excellent toolchain. That's exactly how I feel about it; it's modern. For me, it's like C/C++ but easier to use due to its built-in mechanisms & facilities, packages & modules system, formatting w/tools,

I feel so lucky that, not only have I found something in life that I crave and genuinely love, but I am blessed with the ability to learn it on my by simply reading documentation, manuals, or other published, non-fiction literature. I find most video tutorials on Youtube or the like to be either only good for beginner fundamentals or not good for anything at all.
